## Problem

Khmer ASR research lags behind high-resource languages because of limited transcribed speech data. Most existing systems are proprietary, and open-source alternatives have high word error rates on naturalistic Khmer speech.

## Approach

The thesis focuses on two contributions: (1) a reproducible data preparation pipeline for Khmer speech, and (2) fine-tuning Whisper on the resulting dataset.

### Data Preparation

Collecting sufficient transcribed Khmer audio is the hardest part. The pipeline combines:

- **Real recordings**: sourced from publicly available Khmer broadcast media and read-speech datasets
- **TTS augmentation**: a Khmer TTS engine generates additional training samples from Khmer text corpora, expanding coverage of rare vocabulary and phoneme combinations
- **Preprocessing**: all audio resampled to 16kHz mono, silence trimming, normalization
- **Cleaning**: forced alignment used to flag and remove low-quality transcription pairs

### Fine-tuning Whisper

Whisper's multilingual checkpoints (small, medium) were fine-tuned using HuggingFace's `Seq2SeqTrainer`:

- Khmer-specific tokenizer extension to cover Unicode Khmer script edge cases
- Gradient checkpointing for memory efficiency
- Evaluation with WER on a native-speaker held-out test set

## Results

- Significant WER reduction compared to the zero-shot Whisper baseline on Khmer
- TTS augmentation improved performance on unseen vocabulary
- The data pipeline and model checkpoints are documented for future research reuse

## Key Findings

Low-resource ASR benefits more from data diversity than data volume alone. A small set of high-quality, phonetically diverse recordings outperforms a larger noisy corpus in downstream WER.
