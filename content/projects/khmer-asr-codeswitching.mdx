## Context

Khmer speakers, especially in urban and professional settings, frequently switch between Khmer and English within a single sentence. This code-switching behavior challenges standard monolingual ASR systems, which are trained on one language at a time.

This was my research focus during an internship at the IDIR Lab at CADT.

## Research Questions

1. How do wav2vec2 and Whisper perform out-of-the-box on code-switched Khmer–English speech?
2. What fine-tuning strategies improve multilingual ASR on low-resource code-switched data?
3. What are the key failure modes — mispronunciation, transliteration, language boundary errors?

## Approach

- Collected and annotated a small corpus of naturally occurring code-switched speech
- Benchmarked Whisper (multilingual) and wav2vec2 (multilingual) in zero-shot settings
- Explored targeted fine-tuning on the code-switched subset
- Error analysis: categorized mistakes by language boundary, phoneme confusion, and out-of-vocabulary terms

## Findings

- Whisper's multilingual checkpoint handles English insertions into Khmer reasonably well; the main failures occur at language boundaries with transliterated words
- Wav2Vec2 requires task-specific fine-tuning to reach comparable performance
- Data scarcity is the primary bottleneck — synthetic augmentation via TTS for both languages shows promise

## Stack

- HuggingFace Transformers (Whisper, Wav2Vec2)
- PyTorch
- Python audio processing (librosa, soundfile)
